About NetGuardians

We believe you should know exactly what this extension does and doesn't do with your data.

AI Privacy Advisor — API Key

Enter your API key to unlock AI-powered analysis on the Summary tab. The key is stored locally on your device only and never sent anywhere except the AI provider you choose.

What permissions does this extension use?

Active Tab To analyze the scripts, cookies, and trackers on the page you're currently viewing.
Storage To save your whitelist and preferences locally on your device.
Tabs To detect when you navigate to a new page and update the analysis.

Does my data leave my device?

Mostly no. All tracker detection and risk scoring happens entirely within your browser — no page content or raw browsing data is ever sent anywhere. The one exception is the optional AI Privacy Advisor: when you click "Ask AI", a short summary of the analysis results (risk score, tracker names, and domain) is sent to the Groq API to generate a plain-English explanation. No page content, cookies, or personal information is included. You are always in control — the AI feature only runs when you explicitly request it.

What is stored on my device?

Only two things are stored locally using Chrome's storage API:

Your whitelisted domains (so trusted sites aren't re-analyzed)
Your preference settings (demo mode on/off)

Analysis results are temporary and cleared when you close the tab. No browsing history is recorded.

How does the analysis work?

NetGuardians scans the page for browser-observable signals: third-party scripts, known tracker domains, cookies, session replay tools, and fingerprinting indicators. It compares these against a built-in database of known tracking companies and uses heuristic rules to generate a risk score. Optionally, the AI Privacy Advisor feature sends a brief summary of these results to Groq's API (running Llama 3.3), which translates the findings into a plain-English explanation tailored to what was detected — reducing the cognitive effort required to understand your privacy exposure.

Is the source code available?

NetGuardians is an open project built for the Intro to Human-Centred AI course at Johns Hopkins University (EN.601.494). All code is inspectable in the extension files.
